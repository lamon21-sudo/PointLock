Business Model & Strategic Plan: "PickRivals"

A Social, Skill-Based PvP Prediction Platform

1. Executive Summary

The Product
"PickRivals" (working title) is a mobile-first competitive sports prediction game that bridges the gap between season-long fantasy sports and traditional sports betting. Unlike sportsbooks where users bet against the "house," PickRivals is a peer-to-peer (PvP) platform where users build prediction slips (parlays, props, spreads) to compete directly against friends or the global community. The platform utilizes a virtual currency economy, removing real-money wagering risks while retaining the thrill of "skin in the game."

The Market
We target the "Social Sports Fan" (Gen Z and Millennials)‚Äîa demographic that craves the adrenaline of sports betting but seeks the social connectivity of platforms like Sleeper and Discord. This segment is often alienated by the solitary, transactional nature of traditional sportsbooks or restricted by local gambling laws. By positioning as a skill-based game, PickRivals opens up a nationwide (40+ states) Total Addressable Market (TAM) significantly larger than the regulated betting market.

The Business Model
Revenue is driven by a "Freemium + Rake" model. Users purchase virtual currency (Tokens) to enter skill-based contests. The platform monetizes through:

Token Sales: Direct purchase of game currency.

Contest Rake: A small percentage fee (e.g., 5-10%) taken from prize pools in competitive matches.

High-Margin Utilities: Selling 100% margin digital items like Streak Freezes and Boosts.

Subscription (SaaS): A "Pro" tier offering advanced analytics and cosmetic perks.

3. Legal & Compliance Explanation

Positioning: Skill-Based Social Prediction
PickRivals is not a gambling app; it is a skill-based competition platform.

Why it is not "Gambling"

In most US jurisdictions, gambling requires three elements: Prize, Consideration (Entry Fee), and Chance.

Removing "Chance": Unlike slots or roulette, sports prediction is widely recognized (in fantasy sports legislation) as a game of skill. Success depends on knowledge of player stats, matchups, weather, and injuries.

Removing the "House": In a sportsbook, the house sets the line to beat the player. In PickRivals, the house is a passive host. Player A competes against Player B. The house takes a fee regardless of who wins.

Virtual Currency Isolation:

Inflow: Users buy "Rival Coins" (Entertainment value).

Gameplay: Users wager Coins.

Outflow: Coins cannot be converted back to cash directly.

Compliance Best Practices

No Guaranteed Odds: We do not act as the bookmaker. We provide data; users make decisions.

Geofencing: Block access in states with strict prohibitions on paid-entry fantasy/skill games (typically WA, MT, ID, NV, and occasionally others depending on current legislation).

Age Gating: Strict 18+ (or 19/21+ depending on state) KYC verification using tools like Veriff or Jumio.

Terms of Service: Explicit disclaimer that "Past performance does not guarantee future results" and "Practice on PickRivals does not imply success in real-money gambling."

4. Game Design & User Experience

Core User Flows

1. The "Slip Builder" (The Core Loop)

User selects a sport (e.g., NFL).

UI presents "Cards" for upcoming games.

User taps a market: Moneyline (Winner), Spread (Handicap), or Props (Mahomes Over 280.5 Yards).

User chains these into a "Slip" (similar to a Parlay).

Smart Assist: The app calculates a "Point Potential" based on the difficulty (odds) of the slip.

2. The Challenge Mode (PvP)

Direct Challenge: User sends the Slip to a friend via iMessage/Link. "I bet 1000 Coins I score more points than you."

Public Matchmaking: User posts the Slip to the "Arena." The system pairs them with a user of similar Skill Rating (MMR).

The contest: Both users put up 1000 Coins. Winner takes 1900 Coins (100 Coin Rake).

3. Live Game Experience

Immersive Tracking: Users see their slip vs. their opponent's slip side-by-side.

Live Updates: As Mahomes throws a pass, the progress bar on the "Over 280.5" prop fills up in real-time.

Rewards: End-of-season rewards include exclusive Avatar Frames and large Coin bonuses.

3. The Rake (Revenue)

Standard 1v1: 5% per user (10% total pot).

Math: User A bets $10 (10k RC), User B bets $10 (10k RC). Pot = 20k RC. Winner gets 18k RC. House burns 2k RC.

Tournaments: 10-15% rake on total prize pool.

6. Technical & Data Architecture

High-Level Stack

Frontend: React Native (iOS/Android) for a single codebase with native performance.

Backend: Node.js or Go (Golang) for high-concurrency websocket handling.

Database:

PostgreSQL: For transactional integrity (User Balances, Ledger).

Redis: For live leaderboards and real-time odds caching.

Firestore: For chat logs and social feeds.

Data Strategy

Primary Provider: SportRadar or OddsAPI. We need:

Pre-match odds (Moneyline, Spread, Total).

Player Props (Requires higher tier API).

Live Scoring (Websockets).

Settlement Engine: Automated script that listens to the "Game Final" webhooks from the data provider and triggers the "Payout" function for all active slips.

Key Components

Wallet Service: Handles RC transactions, IAP validation (Apple/Google receipts), and secure ledger.

Matchmaking Engine: Queues users based on:

Stake Amount (1k vs 10k match).

Skill Rating (ELO).

Latency/Region.

Risk Engine: Monitors for collusion (User A always losing to User B to transfer coins) and arbitrage attempts.

8. Risk Analysis & Mitigation

1. Regulatory Risk

Risk: A state attorney general reclassifies the specific economy model as gambling.

Mitigation:

Obtain a formal legal opinion letter (memo) from a gaming law firm before launch.

Keep "Free to Play" (daily free coins) viable so purchase isn't strictly required to play (Alternative Method of Entry).

Maintain distinct "Paid" vs "Free" coin buckets if necessary.

2. Platform Risk (Apple/Google)

Risk: App Store rejection due to "Contests" policy (Guideline 5.3).

Mitigation:

Strictly adhere to the "Skill Gaming" submission guidelines.

Apple Pay integration for token purchases (legitimizes the transaction).

Clearly categorize as "Sports" or "Entertainment," not "Casino."

3. Data Integrity & Cheating

Risk: Users exploiting slow data feeds (courtsiding) to bet on plays that already happened.

Mitigation:

Implement a 6-10 second "Bet Delay" on live contests.

Use "Official League Data" tiers for fastest settlement.

Void matches where data errors occurred.

4. Collusion / Money Laundering

Risk: Users dumping chips to friends using stolen credit cards.

Mitigation:

Closed-loop economy (prizes sent only to verified emails).

Velocity limits on how many matches you can play against the same opponent in 24 hours.
